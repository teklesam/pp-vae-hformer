\caption[PP-VAE-Hformer overall architecture]{%
  \textbf{PP-VAE-Hformer encoder--decoder architecture.}
  A noisy chest radiograph ($1{\times}256{\times}256$) enters a stem convolution
  and three encoder stages (\textbf{E1--E3}), each comprising two HybridBlocks
  followed by strided downsampling; channel depth expands
  $64\!\to\!128\!\to\!256$\,ch while spatial resolution halves.
  The Bottleneck applies two further HybridBlocks at $512{\times}32^2$.
  For probabilistic arms (E--H), the VAE Bottleneck (dashed) maps features to
  a spatial latent via reparameterisation; Arms\,A--D bypass this stage (grey
  dashed arrow).
  Three symmetric decoder stages (\textbf{D3--D1}) upsample and concatenate
  encoder skip features $s_1$--$s_3$ (blue dashed arrows; blue square icons show
  real convolutional feature maps sampled at each spatial scale).
  A dual $3{\times}3$ head produces the denoised mean $\hat{\mu}_y$ and aleatoric
  log-variance $\hat{\sigma}^2_a$.
  Epistemic uncertainty $\hat{\sigma}^2_e$ is the pixel-wise variance of
  $K{=}20$ stochastic forward passes (purple branch).}
\label{fig:arch:overview}
