\caption[HybridBlock (HB) internal structure]{%
  \textbf{HybridBlock (HB) -- sequential convolutional and windowed
  self-attention.}
  The input feature map $\mathbf{f}_{\mathrm{in}}$ first enters the
  \textbf{ResBlock}: two $3{\times}3$ convolution--GroupNorm--GELU sequences
  with a residual shortcut produce $\mathbf{f}_{\mathrm{res}}$ (blue square icon).
  This is then processed by the \textbf{WindowAttnBlock}: after LayerNorm, the
  map is partitioned into non-overlapping $8{\times}8$ windows
  ($H{\times}W{\times}C \to N{\times}64{\times}C$; right panel inset), within
  which four-head multi-head self-attention is applied, followed by a second
  LayerNorm and a two-layer FFN (channel expansion $4{\times}$, GELU).
  An outer residual (purple dashed arrow, right) adds $\mathbf{f}_{\mathrm{res}}$
  directly to the WindowAttn output, yielding $\mathbf{f}_{\mathrm{out}}$.
  Two HybridBlocks are stacked at each encoder, decoder, and bottleneck stage.
  All thumbnails use the same radiograph sample to illustrate the
  identity-preserving residual connections.}
