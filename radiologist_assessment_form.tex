%% ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
%%  BACKGROUND
%% ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
{\small
\textbf{Background.}
Paediatric chest X-rays (CXR) are frequently acquired at reduced radiation dose, resulting in increased quantum noise that may obscure diagnostic features.
Deep-learning denoising methods can recover image quality but risk suppressing or hallucinating pathological findings.
This study evaluates a \emph{Pathology-Preserving Variational Autoencoder with Hformer backbone} (PP-VAE-Hformer), trained on the Kermany paediatric CXR dataset [1]
under a Poisson-Gaussian noise model.
Automated metrics (PSNR, SSIM, LPIPS) assess pixel-level fidelity; however, expert perceptual assessment is required to validate diagnostic acceptability.
The scoring protocol follows the Expert Evaluation of Denoised Noise (EEDN) forced-choice framework [3], adapted for radiological use.
For each pair the \textbf{reference (high-quality)} image is shown first; the reconstruction is shown alongside it.
}

\begin{tabular}{>{\bfseries\centering}p{0.6cm}p{14.2cm}}
  1   & Reconstruction is \textbf{diagnostically equivalent} to the reference: all relevant features (lung markings, cardiac silhouette, consolidation/infiltrates if present) are preserved at the same level of diagnostic confidence. \\[4pt]
  0.5 & Reconstruction is \textbf{diagnostically acceptable} with minor degradation: image quality is slightly reduced but would not change clinical management. \\[4pt]
  0   & Reconstruction is \textbf{diagnostically unacceptable}: significant blurring, artefacts, or loss of pathological features that would alter clinical interpretation. \\
