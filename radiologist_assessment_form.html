PP-VAE-Hformer Paediatric CXR Denoising

MPhil Dissertation Study · University of Cambridge · 2026

BSU
Logo
Background. Paediatric chest X-rays (CXR) are frequently acquired at reduced radiation dose, resulting in increased quantum noise that may obscure diagnostic features. Deep-learning denoising methods can recover image quality but risk suppressing or hallucinating pathological findings. This study evaluates a Pathology-Preserving Variational Autoencoder with Hformer backbone (PP-VAE-Hformer), trained on the Kermany paediatric CXR dataset [1] under a Poisson-Gaussian noise model. Automated metrics (PSNR, SSIM, LPIPS) assess pixel-level fidelity; however, expert perceptual assessment is required to validate diagnostic acceptability. The scoring protocol follows the Expert Evaluation of Denoised Noise (EEDN) forced-choice framework [3], adapted for radiological use.
Scoring Rubric — for each image pair (Reference shown first | Reconstruction shown second):
1Reconstruction is diagnostically equivalent to the reference: all relevant features (lung markings, cardiac silhouette, consolidation/infiltrates if present) are preserved at the same level of diagnostic confidence.
0.5Reconstruction is diagnostically acceptable with minor degradation: image quality is slightly reduced but would not change clinical management.
0Reconstruction is diagnostically unacceptable: significant blurring, artefacts, or loss of pathological features that would alter clinical interpretation.
