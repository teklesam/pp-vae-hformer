%% ─── BACKGROUND ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
{\small
\textbf{Background.}
Paediatric chest X-rays (CXR) are frequently acquired at reduced radiation dose, producing
increased quantum noise that can obscure diagnostic features.
This study evaluates six deep-learning denoising methods trained on the Kermany paediatric
CXR dataset~[1] under a Poisson-Gaussian noise model ($\eta=200$, mid-noise level).
The scoring follows the EEDN forced-choice paradigm~[2]: for each pair the
\textbf{Reference} (clean, high-quality) image is shown first and the \textbf{Reconstruction}
is shown alongside it.
\textbf{Method identities are blinded} — each method is labelled P/Q/R/S/T/U.
The key will be revealed after all assessments are complete.

\textbf{Cases:} 6 representative test images were selected (3 Normal, 3 Pneumonia)
spanning easy / medium / hard reconstruction difficulty (stratified by PSNR decile).
Each case is assessed against all 6 blinded methods (36 scored panels).
Three panels are repeated at the end as intra-rater reliability checks (rows 37--39).
}

\textbf{Scoring Rubric} \textemdash{} for each pair
(\textbf{Reference LEFT} $\mid$ \textbf{Reconstruction RIGHT}):
\vspace{4pt}

\begin{tabular}{>{\bfseries\centering}p{0.6cm}p{14.0cm}}
  1   & \textbf{Diagnostically equivalent}: all features (lung markings, cardiac silhouette,
        consolidation/infiltrates if present) preserved at same diagnostic confidence. \\[3pt]
  0.5 & \textbf{Diagnostically acceptable}: minor degradation; would not change clinical
        management or diagnostic conclusion. \\[3pt]
  0   & \textbf{Diagnostically unacceptable}: blurring, artefacts, or feature loss that would
        alter clinical interpretation. \\
\end{tabular}
\end{mdframed}
